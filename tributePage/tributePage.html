The Life of Ignaz Semmelweis

  • Ignaz Semmelweis was born on 1 July 1818 in Buda, Hungary, today part of Budapest.
  • Ignaz Semmelweis studied medicine at the University of Vienna. He was awarded his doctorate degree in medicine in 1844.
  • Semmelweis specialized in obstetrics.
  • Semmelweis discovered that mortality of mothers due to childbed fever could be drasticaly cut by the use of hand disinfection in the obstetrical clinics.
  • His ideas were rejected by the medical community at the time and gained widespread acceptance only years after his death.
  • In 1865, Semmelweis was committed to an asylum, where he died at age 47 of pyaemia, after being beaten by the guards, only 14 days after he was committed.
